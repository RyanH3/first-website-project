The Inspiration

It all started when I visited my auntie's house. I came to make a serious business deal. I was trading a bag of Cheddar Chex Mix to my cousin for 3 containers of Lay's Stax Potato Crisps. Despite the importance of this deal, my cousin's dog kept charging at us and demanding attention.

We could only hold out for so long. Eventually, we tabled the trade so we could play with him. After about 30 minutes I got tired, so I started to walk back to the house. What I didn't realize was that the dog was not ready to go back inside. He kept running around the neighborhood, forcing me to chase after him. When I caught up, I tried picking him up and carrying him to the house bodily. That worked until he wriggled free and started running again. I caught up to him and tried to tell him that it was time to go. The problem was that he was too excited to talk. Eventually I ended up kneeling down and looking him in the eyes to tell him "We need to go back inside!" He paused for a second. I couldn't tell if he got the message, but what I could see was that he looked really cool. I never saw him as anything but a playful ball of fur until I got up close. I then saw the same thing in other animals I came across. I already enjoyed looking at animals, but only through pictures and videos. I figured other people would enjoy seeing local animals too, so I decided to take pictures of some.

The Goal

My purpose was to get a nice looking picture of an animal that lived near me. I hoped to accomplish it within a year. The due date was not very strict. I had plenty of animals in my neighborhood. I thought the easiest picture to get would, surprisingly, be one of a bird. There were at least three species of bird that lived in my neighborhood. I figured I could probably sneak up on one if I was careful. I even thought I could somehow coax one to approach me! If I could not get a bird, i still had the squirrels and armadillos to rely on. In a desperate situation, I figured I could maybe get a picture of a snake, or a raccoon. In the worst case, I knew I could get a picture of a lizard, or some insect.

The Conflict

My mission is not as easy as I thought. Three times in the past three days, I had the opportunity to get a picture, and I failed by a wide margin. The first attempt was a smll bird, about 18 inches tall. I spotted it eating from my mother's garden, and figured now was my chance to get a picture. As I approached the door to go outside, the bird had already noticed me and ran almost 15 feet away. I was a bit disappointed, but I did not give up immediately. I slowly approached them, pulling out my phone to take a picture. By time I opened the camera app, the bird had flown off.

The bird attempt was a disappointment, but I saw a squirrel on a nearby tree and decided to try to get a picture of them instead. I noticed the squirrel was less squirrelly than the bird. I was approaching them, and they weren't immediately running away! Maybe because they were eating at the time. Nevertheless, I already had my camera on, so I started recording as I approached. I almost got a good shot, but the squirrel ran away too in the end.

Just today, I tried to get a picture of a duck, but it was 50 feet into a lake already, and my zoom quality was not good enough for an observer to even know that it was a duck if they were watching the video I took. I hoped it would swim toward me (for no reason), but it only swam away.

Possible Solution

All my attempts have been fails, but I have not run out of options yet! I still have other animals to approach like lizards, armadillos, snakes, and raccoons. If they all refuse to participate, I might resort to setting my phone down and hoping an animal approaches it, but also does not attack it and does not take it. If that doesn't work, I will turn my focus to insects. I have seen some beautiful white moths and some viscious red ants. If all else fails, I will just take some photos of plants.
